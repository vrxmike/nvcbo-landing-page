Our Core Programs

Northern Vision CBO implements four flagship programs that address interconnected challenges of climate change, gender inequality, education gaps and peace and security. Healing circles and storytelling cut across all programs as core engagement and learning tools.

Climate Adaptation & Food Security

Goal: To strengthen community resilience to climate change through sustainable agriculture, renewable energy, ecosystem restoration, and locally led adaptation solutions.
  • Community Farm & Circular Economy Model learning center integrating solar-powered water pumping, fish farming (aquaculture), and Azolla breeding.
  • Regreening & Ecosystem Restoration Mobilizing communities for tree planting (indigenous and fruit trees) in schools and homesteads.
  • Renewable Energy Exploring solar and wind systems to improve energy access and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
Healing Circles: Used to co-design adaptation solutions, reflect on climate impacts, and strengthen community ownership.

Education & Culture

Goal: To expand access to education, mentorship and cultural knowledge while strengthening identity, leadership, and intergenerational learning.
  • Youth Mentorship & Learning Providing exposure and learning opportunities for young people, especially girls and marginalized youth.
  • Cultural Education & Heritage Documenting indigenous knowledge and cultural practices through storytelling and creative media.
Healing Circles: Used for mentorship, intergenerational knowledge sharing, youth leadership reflection, and cultural dialogue.

Sexual & Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR)

Goal: To promote dignity, health, and informed decision-making among adolescents and women through community-based education and advocacy.
  • Menstrual Health Education Sessions on hygiene and stigma reduction while distributing sanitary products.
  • Community Dialogue on Harmful Practices Addressing Female Genital Cutting (FGC) and gender norms through culturally grounded dialogues.
  • Adolescent Empowerment Building knowledge and leadership in young girls, linking SRHR to future livelihoods.
Healing Circles: Providing safe spaces for sensitive conversations on gender, health, dignity and rights.
